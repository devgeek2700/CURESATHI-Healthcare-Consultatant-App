
1. What are the early signs of COVID-19?
Elevated body temperature, persistent dry cough, respiratory distress, fatigue, body aches, loss of taste or smell, and potential gastrointestinal symptoms like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

2. What are the symptoms of Influenza?
Fever, chills, persistent cough with phlegm, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, muscle or body aches, and profound fatigue.

3. What are the symptoms of coronary artery disease?
Angina (chest pain or discomfort), shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeat, and dizziness.

4. What are the indicators of Type 2 Diabetes?
Increased thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, and blurred vision due to altered insulin utilization.

5. How does a stroke manifest?
Sudden numbness or weakness, confusion, difficulty speaking or understanding speech, and problems with walking or coordination.

6. What are common symptoms of cancer?
Unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, changes in the skin, alterations in bowel or bladder habits, and a lasting cough, depending on the type.

7. What are the signs of neurodegenerative disorders like Alzheimer's disease?
Memory loss, confusion, difficulties in problem-solving, and mood or personality changes.

8. Describe the symptoms of asthma.
Shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing (often worse at night), and chest tightness.

9. What are the symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis?
Joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and fatigue.

10. How does osteoporosis affect individuals?
Bone pain, fractures (especially in the hip, spine, or wrist), and loss of height over time due to compression fractures in the spine.

11. What may indicate hypertension?
Often asymptomatic but may lead to headaches, shortness of breath, and, in severe cases, nosebleeds.

12. What are early symptoms of HIV/AIDS?
Fever, fatigue, muscle aches, and swollen lymph nodes, progressing to opportunistic infections and complications affecting various organs.

13. What medications are commonly used to treat Type 2 Diabetes?
Metformin, Sulfonylureas, DPP-4 inhibitors, and GLP-1 receptor agonists.

14. How is hypertension managed?
Medications like ACE inhibitors, Beta-blockers, Calcium channel blockers, and Diuretics, along with lifestyle modifications.

15. What treatments are used for asthma?
Inhaled corticosteroids, Bronchodilators, and Leukotriene modifiers, along with avoiding triggers and monitoring symptoms.

16. How is depression treated?
Medications like SSRIs, SNRIs, and Tricyclic antidepressants, along with psychotherapy and lifestyle changes.

17. What medications are used for osteoporosis?
Bisphosphonates, Denosumab, and Hormone therapy (in postmenopausal women).

18. What medications are prescribed for coronary artery disease?
Aspirin, Beta-blockers, Statins, and Antiplatelet drugs, along with lifestyle changes.

19. How are infectious diseases prevented?
Good hygiene, vaccinations, and safe behaviors to prevent the spread of infections.

20. What are the first aid measures for a heart attack?
Call emergency services, assist the person in sitting down, keep them calm, encourage deep breaths, and initiate CPR if trained.

21. What are the first aid measures for a stroke?
Call emergency services, keep the person comfortable, and avoid giving them anything to eat or drink.

22. How should one respond to seizures?
Ensure the person's safety, move away hazardous objects, place them on their side, and time the seizure. Call for medical help if needed.

23. What are the first aid measures for choking?
Encourage forceful coughing, perform abdominal thrusts if necessary, and call emergency services if the person cannot breathe or cough.

24. How should bleeding be addressed?
Apply direct pressure on the wound, elevate the injured limb if possible, and call emergency services for severe bleeding.

25. What is the recommended first aid for a diabetic emergency (hypoglycemia)?
Provide a sugary drink or snack, recheck blood sugar levels, and seek medical help if symptoms persist or worsen.
